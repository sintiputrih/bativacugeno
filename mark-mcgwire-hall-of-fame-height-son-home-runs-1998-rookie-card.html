    Mark McGwire is an American former professional baseball first baseman. He was born on October 1, 1963, in Pomona, California.

    His Major League Baseball (MLB) playing career spanned from 1986 to 2001 while playing for the Oakland Athletics and the St. Louis Cardinals, winning one World Series championship each, with Oakland as a player in 1989.

    McGwire was one of several central figures in baseball’s steroids scandal. In 2010, McGwire publicly admitted to using performance-enhancing drugs during a large portion of his career.

    Mark McGwire Hall Of Fame

    Mark McGwire remains an Oakland A’s legend. Considered to be the best player in the franchise history that has been inducted into the Hall of Fame, he is still remembered for his impressive home runs and the formidable pair he and Jose Canseco were in the middle of the lineup in the 1980s into the 1990s.
