    Jade Mathis On Mathis Family Matters

    E!’s new reality TV series Mathis Family Matters premiered on June 19, 2022, and the show featured the daily life of the honorable judge Greg Mathis, his wife Linda Mathis, and their kids, Greg Jr, Camara, Amir, and our star, Jade Mathis.

    Well, though some may be seeing Jade’s father Greg for the first time, the judge is certainly not new to the reality TV scene. Prior to this, he was the face of the Emmy Award-winning television court show Judge Mathis. However, Mathis Family Matters was definitely the first show which followed all the Mathis family members.

    “Excited to share this moment with you all. Don’t miss #MathisFamilyMatters premiering June 19th only on @eentertainment,” Jade promoted Mathis Family Matters over her IG.

    Also, equally excited were her siblings/co-stars, Camara Mathis Webb (second child, a lawyer, a wife, and mother to two beautiful daughters), Greg Mathis Jr. (third child, a.k.a. “Bobby”, and a real estate agent who ventured with his boyfriend, Elliott), and Amir Mathis (the youngest, an established television producer and a talented networker).

    For those who haven’t heard about Jade’s mother, Linda is a teacher and an entrepreneur who transitioned her career to dedicate all her time to focus on the children, household, and family life. She married Judge Greg right after graduating from college and welcomed their brood in Detroit, Mich. In 2005, the Mathis family then moved to Los Angeles and resided there ever since.

    Jade Mathis Job

    Jade Mathis is an attorney, public speaker, and mental health advocate located in the Washington D.C. area. She founded The Resilient JEM, LLC in October 2021 to educate marginalized minorities on how to own their story and excel, provoke resilience, and empower them to execute strategic plans that will accelerate their lives.

    According to Jade, her initiatives included continued work on criminal justice reform, motivational speaking, and advocacy for mental health awareness.

    Following the footsteps of her father, and also after receiving a life-changing mental health diagnosis while in college, Jade became determined to change the way the world views mental health. Since then, she’s captured the attention of millions across the world, not only by sharing her story of resilience and perseverance but also with her bold, authentic, unapologetic, and unashamed approach to addressing mental health.

    Dubbed the “Expungement Guru,” Jade has also firsthand observed the disparities and deficits in the criminal justice system and created diversion programs as alternatives to incarceration.

    Prior to this, Jade worked as an Assistant State’s Attorney for Prince George’s County Maryland for 7 long years. At the end of her tenure in Oct 2021, Jade then took it to her IG to write, “The most valuable lesson I’ve learned working on the frontlines of the Criminal Justice System is sometimes justice isn’t incarceration, its reform, and rehabilitation. Being a nontraditional prosecutor has been one of my proudest achievements. Although today is my last day serving in this capacity, it is not my last day fighting for the voiceless and advocating for reform.”

    Jade Mathis Disability

    Jade Mathis was diagnosed with depression and ADHD in 2006. She was in her sophomore year of college studying broadcast journalism at a university in Georgia then.

    “I was at home in bed all day with the windows (and) the blinds closed, just in the dark, random crying and overwhelming sadness,” she recalled. Also, since her grades were taking a hit at school, she opened up to her father about it.

    Judge Greg then immediately asked to go see a psychologist. And so she did.

    After being diagnosed, Jade then learned to “protect her ears.” Not literally but as to what she listens to and what she watches. “I was a news junkie, but I had to pull back from the news. I had to pull back from certain reality shows,” she explained.

    Also, Jade learned to advocate on her own behalf, and over time, she found herself helping people around her struggling with mental health as well. Her one piece of advice to people struggling with themself is to be resilient and be able to bounce back. 

    Now, Jade’s thankful for her invisible disability, for it has led her to be able to help many like her.
