    Early Life and Education

    Robert Metcalfe was born in Brooklyn, New York, and showed an early interest in technology. He pursued his passion by studying electrical engineering and computer science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and later at Harvard University, where he earned his Ph.D.

    Professional Career

    Metcalfe’s professional career began at the renowned Xerox PARC, where he co-invented Ethernet. He later co-founded 3Com Corporation, a company that specialized in Ethernet technology and network equipment, contributing significantly to his wealth.

    Co-Invention of Ethernet

    Ethernet, a technology that allows computers to communicate over a local area network (LAN), was a groundbreaking invention. It revolutionized the tech industry and is still widely used today. Metcalfe’s role in its invention significantly boosted his professional reputation and wealth.

    Founding of 3Com Corporation

    After leaving Xerox, Metcalfe co-founded 3Com Corporation in 1979. The company became a leading provider of networking equipment, further increasing Metcalfe’s wealth when it was sold to Hewlett-Packard for $2.7 billion in 2010.

    Awards and Recognition
