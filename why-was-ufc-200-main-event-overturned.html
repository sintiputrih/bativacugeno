    Why was UFC 200 Main Event overturned?

    UFC 200, a highly anticipated event in the mixed martial arts (MMA) world, was marred by controversy due to the main event involving Brock Lesnar and Mark Hunt. 

    The Background of UFC 200

    The build-up to UFC 200 was marked by a series of unfortunate events, casting a shadow over the much-anticipated event.

    The main event was initially planned to feature Jon Jones, the top-ranked pound-for-pound fighter, in a title rematch with Daniel Cormier.

    But, a mid-June drug-testing sample from Jones was flagged by the United States Anti-Doping Agency (USADA), leading to his removal from the main event.

    In light of Jones' removal, the focus shifted to a three-round heavyweight bout between Brock Lesnar, who had spent the last four-plus years outside the Octagon in WWE, and Mark Hunt as the new top-shelf attraction of UFC 200.

    The fight, which was expected to add star power to the event, eventually resulted in a legal battle and a major controversy that shook the MMA world.

    The Fight and its Fallout

    The UFC 200 fight between Mark Hunt and Brock Lesnar took place in 2016. Lesnar emerged victorious, securing a win by unanimous decision. 

    But the victory was short-lived as Lesnar's drug tests returned positive for multiple banned substances, prompting the U.S. Anti-Doping Agency and the Nevada Athletic Commission to suspend Lesnar.

    This turn of events led to a legal battle as Mark Hunt filed a lawsuit against the UFC, Dana White, and Brock Lesnar.

    Hunt alleged that White and the UFC were aware of Lesnar's use of performance-enhancing drugs (PEDs) but encouraged Lesnar to fight him at UFC 200 despite this knowledge.

    The lawsuit brought to light serious allegations of doping and raised questions about the UFC's handling of the situation.

    The Lawsuit and Legal Battle

    The lawsuit filed by Mark Hunt against the UFC, Dana White, and Brock Lesnar resulted in a prolonged legal battle. The case was initially dismissed in 2019 before being brought back to court in appeals in 2021

    . After years of legal wrangling, the matter finally reached its conclusion in 2023, with the court granting the defendants' motion for summary judgment on all claims, leading to Hunt losing the lawsuit.

    The legal battle not only shed light on the specific case of Lesnar's doping allegations but also brought into focus broader issues related to the UFC's business practices and its handling of doping infractions.

    The fallout from the lawsuit and the subsequent implications for the UFC's image and legal standing were significant.

    Implications and Fallout

    The overturning of the UFC 200 main event and the subsequent legal battle had far-reaching implications for the MMA world and the UFC in particular.

    The allegations of doping, coupled with the legal battle, cast a shadow over the integrity of the sport and the UFC's regulatory processes.

    The case also highlighted the challenges and complexities associated with maintaining fair play and ethical standards in combat sports.

    Furthermore, the legal battle and the subsequent judgment had financial implications for the UFC.

    Reports suggested that the company could be required to pay a substantial sum in compensation if it were to lose the lawsuit, with estimates ranging from $800 million to $1.6 billion.

    The financial repercussions of such a judgment could have significant implications for the UFC's operations and long-term financial stability.

    The Future of UFC and Lessons Learned

    The UFC's handling of the UFC 200 main event and the subsequent legal battle with Mark Hunt underscored the need for robust regulatory mechanisms and ethical standards in the sport.

    It also raised questions about the responsibilities of governing bodies and event organizers in ensuring fair competition and maintaining the integrity of the sport.

     

    As the UFC navigates the fallout from the UFC 200 controversy, it is imperative for the organization to reevaluate its policies and practices related to doping, fighter safety, and regulatory oversight. 

    The overturning of the UFC 200 main event involving Brock Lesnar and Mark Hunt was a watershed moment in the history of the UFC and MMA.

    The legal battle, doping allegations, and financial implications underscored the complexities and challenges associated with maintaining the integrity and fairness of combat sports.
