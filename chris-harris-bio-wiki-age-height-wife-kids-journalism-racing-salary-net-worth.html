    Chris Harris Journalism Career

    Harris began his career in an automotive industry where he worked for the ‘Autocar’ magazine. His roles included cleaning ashtrays. After a while, he was promoted to a road-test editor. He was given the role of writing automotive reviews and regular opinion columns.

    In 2008, he left ‘Autocar’ magazine and co-founded a web-based digital platform ‘Drivers Republic’. Unfortunately, one year later, the platform collapsed. He was hired by the ‘Evo’ magazine as a reviewer/writer.

    On October 12, 2009, Harris published his first article. Harris partnered with ‘/DRIVE’. ‘/DRIVE’ is an automotive network and popular YouTube channel and created an opinion and review YouTube series. ‘Chris Harris on Cars,’ began to air weekly. It consisted of 104 videos which together earned over 3.5 million views in just two years.

    In 2014, Harris left ‘/DRIVE” and created his own YouTube channel with the same name, ‘Chris Harris on Cars’. Nonetheless, Harris also appears on the American TV channel ‘NBCSN’. He also reviews cars and takes part in automotive-related activities.

    Chris Harris Automotive Racing and Television Career

    Harris won his first race in 2000 in a Formula Palmer Audi. He also uploads race-related videos on his channel from time to time. Harris joined the cast of the TV series ‘Top Gear’ in 2016.

    When he started, he worked as a recurring presenter. However, as time moved on, he was promoted as the lead presenter after Chris Evans resigned from the show. Harris’ web series titled ‘Chris Harris on Cars’ was launched on the channel ‘BBC America’.

    Chris Harris Controversies

    Harris was banned from reviewing the Ferrari cars in 2011. This was after he wrote an article ‘How Ferrari Spins’. In this article, he criticized the brand and addressed it as ‘profoundly irritating’. In 2013, the ban was lifted.
