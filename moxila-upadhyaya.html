    Moxila Upadhyaya’s Career

    Following law school, Upadhyaya embarked on a two-year clerkship under the Honorable Eric Washington, former chief judge of the D.C. Court of Appeals. This invaluable experience laid the foundation for her subsequent legal career.

    Moxila Upadhyaya’s journey came full circle when she joined the D.C. District Court in 2011 as the first law clerk to the Honorable Robert Wilkins.

    This early exposure to the intricacies of the judicial system proved invaluable. Returning to Venable, she ascended the ranks to become a partner while continuing her litigation practice.

    This phase of her career was defined by her exemplary pro bono work, leading to her recognition as Venable’s Pro Bono Lawyer of the Year in 2006 and the Mid-Atlantic Innocence Project’s Defender of Innocence Award in 2009.

    Beyond her courtroom engagements, Upadhyaya’s commitment extended to various legal and justice organizations. From 2021 to 2022, she was appointed to the D.C. District Court’s Committee on Grievances, demonstrating her dedication to upholding the integrity of legal practice.
