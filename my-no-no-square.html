    Overall Meaning

    The lyrics of Jack Shore's song My No No Square deal with the idea of personal boundaries and the right of every individual to have control over their own body. The song addresses the issue of unwanted touching, especially in the area commonly referred to as the "private parts." The singer repeatedly asserts "No-no, don't touch me there," emphasizing the message that this is a clear and non-negotiable boundary that should be respected.


    The use of the term "no-no square" is an example of a child-friendly way of describing the concept of personal boundaries and emphasizing the importance of consent. The simplicity of the lyrics and their repetitive nature make the song an effective educational tool for parents and teachers to explain these concepts to young children in a way that is easy to understand.
