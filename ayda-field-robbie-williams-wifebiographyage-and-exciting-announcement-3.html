    Ayda Field Biography

    Ayda Field is an American actress and television personality who was born and brought up in Los Angeles, California, United States of America. She is widely known for serving as a regular panellist on British television show Loose Women as well as a judge on the British The X Factor, alongside her husband, English singer-songwriter Robbie Williams.

    Field has appeared in Fresh Meat as Rosa, Strange Wilderness as Mountain Nurse, Days of Our Lives as Angela Moroni, Play Dead as Marissa, Blue Collar TV as Various Characters, Austenland as Molly, and in Back to You as Montana Diaz Herrera.

    Early Life and Education

    Field was born on 17 May 1979, in Los Angeles, California. She was born to a Turkish father, and an American mother, the movie producer Gwen Field. Her father is Muslim and her mother is Jewish.

    Field attended the prestigious Harvard-Westlake School in Los Angeles, where she graduated in 1997. During her time at Harvard-Westlake, she was involved in theater and drama productions, which sparked her interest in acting.

    After graduating from high school, Field enrolled at Brown University, where she studied theater and literature. However, she left Brown after two years to pursue her acting career full-time.

    Rise to Fame

    While still at Brown University, Field landed her first acting role in a low-budget film called “Shopping Spree” in 1999. This minor role gave her a taste of the industry and fueled her determination to pursue acting full-time. After leaving Brown, she moved to Los Angeles and began attending auditions, taking on small roles in television shows like “Everwood” and “Boston Public.”

    Field’s big break came in 2004 when she was cast as Nicki Minaj in the British sitcom “Fresh Meat.” Nicki was a quirky and outspoken character that resonated with audiences, and Field’s portrayal earned her critical acclaim and recognition.
