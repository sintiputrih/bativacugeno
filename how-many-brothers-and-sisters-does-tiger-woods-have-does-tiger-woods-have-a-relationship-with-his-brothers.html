    Tiger Woods holds numerous golf records and ranks second in men’s major championship wins and is tied for first in PGA Tour wins.

    Woods is one of the most well-known athletes of the modern era and is widely regarded as one of the greatest golfers of all time. The World Golf Hall of Fame has chosen to honor him.

    Woods turned professional in 1996 at the age of 20 after a stellar junior, college, and amateur golf career. He had won three PGA Tour events by the end of April 1997 in addition to his first major, the 1997 Masters, which he won by a record-breaking 12 strokes. In June 1997, less than a year after becoming a professional, he made history by becoming number one in the Official World Golf Ranking for the first time.

    Woods dominated the sport of golf for the first ten years of the 21st century. From August 1999 to September 2004 (264 consecutive weeks), and again from June 2005 to October 2010, he topped the world’s golf rankings. He won 13 of golf’s major championships during this time.
