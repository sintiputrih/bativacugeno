    Jimmy Chin is a professional climber, mountaineer, skier, filmmaker, and photographer who has competed on all seven continents. He was born and reared in the Minnesota city of Mankato. Jimmy joined with The North Face in 2001 and has been a member of the North Face Athlete Team for fourteen years. A number of climbing, ski-mountaineering, and exploratory trips have been planned and conducted by him in countries as diverse as China, Pakistan, Nepal; Tanzania; Chad; Mali; South Africa; Tibet; Borneo; India; and Argentina. 

    A few of his notable accomplishments include scaling and skiing Mount Everest’s North Face from the summit, ascents of big walls and alpine towers in the Karakoram Mountains of Pakistan and the Garwhal Himalayas of Northern India, crossing the Chang Tang Plateau in north-western Tibet on foot, and an attempt on the mountain’s direct North Face in alpine style. Jimmy has appeared in a number of publications, both in front of and behind the camera, including National Geographic, Outside, and Men’s Journal, and has been named one of People magazine’s most eligible bachelors. He has also appeared in a number of television shows, including National Geographic and Outside. He graduated from Carleton College with a Bachelor of Arts in Asian Studies.
