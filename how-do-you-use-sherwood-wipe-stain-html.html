    How do you use Sherwood wipe stain?

    Answer

    Spray a complete wet coat over the surface. Use a soft cloth to wipe away any excess discoloration by wiping in a circular motion with the cloth. Allow some of the solvent to flash off for a deeper colour – up to 15 minutes maximum working time – to get a richer hue.

    How would you go about applying wipe stains in this manner?

    Make liberal applications of stain using a brush, lint-free cloth, or spray until the surface is fully saturated. Allow for 5 to 15 minutes of penetration time. Wipe the surface down with a soft cloth, first across the grain and then with it. When the initial layer is dried, re-stain the surface to get a deeper hue.

    Furthermore, are you able to apply two coats of stain?

    The usual rule of thumb is to apply only as much deck stain as the wood can absorb before it starts to peel away from the surface. Unless you’re working with exceptionally solid hardwoods, which may only be able to absorb one layer of wood stain, you’ll often need two applications. More information on how many coats of stain to apply may be found in this YouTube video.

    People have also inquired as to what BAC wiping stain is.

    BAC Wiping Stains are a combination of base stains and formulated colours that provide a wide range of classic and modern wood colour tones for wiping and wiping boards. BAC Wiping Stains are meant only for use in industrial shops and manufacturing facilities.

    Is it necessary to sand between stain layers of paint?

    A word of caution: While sanding between coats is not strictly essential, it will result in a superior surface. After a coat has dried, carefully smooth the surface with 220 or 240 grit sandpaper or extra fine steel wool to remove any remaining imperfections. Sanding leaves a white film on the surface of the finish, which will dissolve as soon as the next coat is applied. Do not sand the final layer after it has been applied.
