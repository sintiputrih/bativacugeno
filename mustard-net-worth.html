    Mustard | Career

    DJ Mustard released his debut studio album entitled 10 Summers on August 11, 2014, which sold over 100,000 copies.

    It peaked at No.20 on the US R&B Hip-hop charts while it made in the top 15 of the US Rap Album charts.

    Moving on, Mustard released yet another studio album entitled Cold Summer on September 16, 2016, following his successful debut studio album.

    Meanwhile, this album did not live up to expectations as it only peaked at No. 24 on the US R&B/HH album charts.

    Moreover, Mustard dropped his third studio album entitled Perfect Ten on June 28, 2019.

    The album was significantly successful than his previous two studio albums.

    It secured the third spot in the US Rap album chart, and peaked at No.5 on the US R&B/HH charts, and ranked at No. 8 on the US Billboard Hot 200.

    Similarly, the album was labeled platinum by RIAA as it sold over 1 million copies in the United States.

    Some Interesting Facts About Mustard

    • Mustard got a taste of DJing at first when he was 11 in the house party organized by his uncle, who also happened to be a DJ at the time.
    • Surprisingly, Dijon McFarlane nicknamed himself DJ Mustard because he was an avid lover of dijon mustard (Dijon mustard is the traditional mustard of France, named after the town of Dijon in Burgundy, France), and since his first name was Dijon, he quickly adapted this name and abbreviating Dijon as DJ.
