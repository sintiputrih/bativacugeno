    Phil Mickelson Clothing Sponsor [Brand Deals]

    Phil Mickelson clothing sponsor brands are Callaway, Mizzen+Main and KPMG. Mickelson is also affiliated with beer company Amstel Light. These brands are known for their high-quality and performance-driven clothing that perfectly aligns with Mickelson's image as a professional athlete.

    16 Jun 2024, 00:00

    Phil Mickelson clothing sponsor brands are Callaway, Mizzen+Main and KPMG. Mickelson is also affiliated with beer company Amstel Light.

    These brands are known for their high-quality and performance-driven clothing that perfectly aligns with Mickelson's image as a professional athlete.

    The three times Masters Tournament champion and two-time PGA Championship winner, Phil, is further associated with several renowned brands for sponsorship. Some other prominent names in the list include Rolex, Melin, VistaJet, Titleist and more.

    Even though he is second on the PGA Tour's money list for tournament winnings, he makes much more money from endorsements than from prizes.

    As of 2023, Mickelson has earned over $96 million in prize money on the PGA Tour. In 2022, he made an estimated $42 million from endorsements, while he only earned $140,608 from prize money.

    1. Mizzen+Main

    Phil Mickelson signed a deal with Mizzen+Main, a performance menswear brand in 2018. He became an ambassador for their products as a part of the deal.

    Mizzen+Main is a clothing company based in America, started by Kevin Lavelle in 2012. They focus on creating performance menswear designed to be comfortable and functional.

    Moreover, under the agreement, Mickelson wears Mizzen and Main shirts during his weekend tours on the golf course. During the 2018 Players Championship, a 6-foot-3 athlete sported a Mizzen+Main long-sleeve dress shirt as part of their partnership agreement.

    As a result of this collaboration, Mickelson not only received cash but also acquired an undisclosed ownership stake in the Mizzen+Main company.

    Besides, the Phil Mickelson apparel sponsor company has one other athlete with an equity stake, namely JJ Watt, a defensive end for the Houston Texans.

    2. Callaway

    Phil Mickelson and Callaway have been close partners for a long time. Callaway, his equipment sponsor has likely been a key factor in his victories in golf.

    Since 2004, Mickelson has been using Callaway equipment, and his success on the golf course has been a great way to promote the brand.

    Phil Mickelson signed a five-year deal with Callaway for about $4 million in 2004. As part of this agreement, the 2013 Open Championship winner started using various golf equipment from Callaway, including the HX golf ball, Fusion driver, Big Bertha Woods, as well as Callaway shoes and gloves.

    Apart from using Callaway clubs, Mickelson has teamed up with the company to create new golf products. One famous example is the Callaway Mack Daddy PM Grind Wedge, which Mickelson helped design. Additionally, the putter that Phil uses on the course is also made by Callaway.

    3. KPMG

    KPMG has been one of Phil Mickelson's main sponsors since 2008. Phil inked a three-year global sponsorship deal with KPMG in February 2008.

    The accounting firm's logo was prominently displayed on Mickelson's headwear, and he was a regular spokesperson for the company.

    What makes their partnership special is that it is more than just about promoting their brand. Mickelson has teamed up with KPMG to support financial education. Together, they have also raised funds for charitable purposes through KPMG's charitable foundation.

    However, in February 2022, KPMG and Mickelson mutually parted ways. This decision was due to his controversial remarks about the Saudi Arabian government and the proposed LIV Golf Invitational Series.

    4. Workday

    Workday and Phil Mickelson had a five-year long-term deal, which they agreed on in 2017. In March 2022, their partnership came to an end.

    Workday is a cloud-based company that provides software and financial management services. They focus on helping businesses with managing their employees and finances.

    Mickelson had been working as a brand ambassador for the company. Besides, he has been in many advertisements for Workday, and the company has also supported some of his events.

    However, in 2022, Workday Inc decided not to continue working with Phil as their brand ambassador because of the comments he made about the PGA Tour and LIV Golf.

    5. ExxonMobil

    Phil Mickelson and ExxonMobil had a sponsor partnership that lasted for over 16 years. It started in 2004 and lasted until the end of 2020. 

    The sponsorship deal was very valuable, reportedly worth $50 million each year. This made it one of the most profitable and significant sponsorship deals ever in golf.

    Additionally, among all on Phil Mickelson sponsor list, the partnership with ExxonMobil was one of the most financially rewarding.

    ExxonMobil featured Mickelson prominently in their marketing campaigns, using him in TV commercials and ads. He was not only a brand ambassador but also represented the company by making public appearances and speaking on its behalf.

    6. Rolex

    Rolex has been Phil Mickelson watch sponsor for two decades since 2004. Mickelson sports a wide variety of Rolex watches during his golf tournaments.

    One of the most decorated golfers with an astounding 45 PGA Tour wins and six major championships, his association with Rolex has helped to boost the brand's profile among golfers and watch enthusiasts alike.

    As a brand ambassador for the Swiss luxury watchmaker, Phil wears an array of classic and more contemporary watches. His style perfectly matches the timeless elegance of Rolex timepieces, and his performance is truly impressive, just like the watches he represents.

    7. Melin

    Phil Mickelson sponsors 2023 feature a premium headwear manufacturing company called Melin. 

    Established in 2015, Melin stands tall as an esteemed American enterprise specializing in crafting high-quality headwear. Notably, in 2020, Melin secured the collaboration with Mickelson as an esteemed brand ambassador.

    This significant partnership between Melin and Mickelson was forged through a far-reaching multi-year agreement and is believed to be worth several million dollars.

    As part of the deal, Mickelson agreed to wear Melin hats on the golf course and in public appearances. He also became a brand ambassador for the company and appeared in Melin's marketing campaigns.

    8. Titleist

    Phil Mickelson made a big agreement with the golf equipment company Titleist in 2000. They signed a contract for five years worth a whopping $20 million.

    Previously Mickelson had partnered with Yonex for his golf kits and tools. For most of his early career, he remained loyal to Titleist. Mickelson is recognized for using the Titleist Pro V1x golf ball.

    But in mid-to-late 2004, the deal with Titleist came to an abrupt end 16 months before it was supposed to. The reason was that Mickelson decided to sign with Callaway instead.

    The new contract with Callaway was said to be around $4 million per year, plus extra bonuses. Despite trying to renegotiate with Titleist, they could not reach an agreement, and Mickelson was allowed to leave the deal early.

    However, Mickelson maintained a close relationship with the company and later co-founded Fox Wellness with Dave Phillips, the co-founder of the Titleist.

    9. VistaJet

    Phil Mickelson signed an endorsement deal with VistaJet in 2020. VistaJet is a multinational private aviation company established in 2004.

    It is one of few brands that remained affiliated with Phil Mickelson Sponsor Masters in 2023. After a year away from golf events, he returned to play for the Masters Tournament.

    Mickelson talked to Forbes and shared that he sold his private jet and started flying with VistaJet. He called it the best decision he had ever made, even though he had owned a plane for two decades.

    He said flying with VistaJet has significantly lowered his stress levels, allowing him to concentrate more on his golf game and spending time with his family.

    10. Amstel Light

    Phil Mickelson forged a substantial marketing partnership with Amstel Light in 2019 for a multiyear engagement.

    Amstel Light is a subordinate company of a Dutch brewery king, Heineken International. As a part of the deal, Mickelson became both an investor and a promotional advocate for their brand.

    Heineken wanted to boost Amstel Light's reputation, as the beer had been losing popularity in the past few years. They believed that Mickelson's fame and influence could be the key to bringing about a positive change and revitalizing the brand.

    The collaboration started with a fresh advertising campaign aimed at reaching male customers from the Gen X generation and older age groups.

    Unfortunately, in 2022, Mickelson's statements about LIV Golf caused a lot of disagreement. As a result, Amstel Light decided to end their association with him, following the lead of their parent company.
