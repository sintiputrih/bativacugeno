    Armand Duplantis is an American-born Swedish pole vaulter and the current world indoor record holder with a height of 6.18 meters and world best(outdoor) of 6.15 meters. He was born on November 10, 1999, in Lafayette, Louisiana, USA. He is the son of Greg Duplantis and Helena, a former heptathlete and volleyball player.

    Armand first tried pole vaulting as a three-year-old old at the family home in Lafayette, Louisiana. In 2015, during his freshman year at the Lafayette High School, Duplantis set national freshmen to record both indoors and outdoors and was named the Gatorade Louisiana Boys Track & Field Athlete of the year.

    On March 6, Armand completed the 2021 European indoor Championships. He was the overwhelming favorite to win the title after the late withdrawal of Renaud Lavillenie with Injury. He, however, set a new championship record of 6.05m before making three unsuccessful attempts at 6.19m, his second narrowly missing the world records.
